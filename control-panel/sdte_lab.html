The Google Maps Analogy

The GPS of Automation

You hit the nail on the head, Natalia. A locator isn't the destination (the data); it is the **address** that tells Playwright exactly where to go in the complex city of the DOM.

1. Locating (Finding)

Like coordinates on a map, `page.locator()` establishes the path. It doesn't mean we are there yet; it just means we know **how** to get there.

// Storing the address
const badge = page.locator('.badge');

2. Interacting (Doing)

Once the address is set, Playwright "drives" to the location and performs an action (click, fill, hover) to change the application's state.

// Arriving and acting
await badge.click();

Why it "Does Things"

Unlike a static variable, a locator has **Intelligence**. If there is "traffic" (the element is loading), Playwright waits. This chart shows how locators improve test stability.
